My goal for today's video is to help you understand how to future proof your knowledge systems for both yourself and for AI, so you don't lose your work, ideas, and knowledge between tools. The solution? A custom wiki.

The LLM Wiki has been gaining popularity lately as a result of Andrej Karpathy's github post, but it's a concept that has been around for years. Knowledge Graphs can significantly improve the quality and efficiency of AI tools as compared to standard RAG (retrieval augmented generation), especially for complex systems.

But... that's a bit complex. It's helpful to start by understanding what a knowledge graph is from a basic level, how it can be used alongside traditional AI query systems, and how you can easily get started with building yours today in Obsidian.
